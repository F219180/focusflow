The Science of Attention

Sustained attention is the brain's ability to maintain focus on a single task over an extended period without switching or drifting. It depends on the prefrontal cortex ‚Äî the brain's executive control center ‚Äî which regulates impulses and guides voluntary focus.

When we habitually consume short-form content, the brain adapts to expect rapid novelty and instant reward signals. Over time, the dopaminergic reward system recalibrates, making slow, continuous tasks feel unrewarding. Academic work ‚Äî reading chapters, attending lectures ‚Äî suddenly feels "too hard" not because it is, but because the brain has been retrained to resist it.

The good news? Neuroplasticity means this process is reversible through deliberate, structured practice.
